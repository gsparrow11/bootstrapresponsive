My Top 5 Favorite Dinosaurs

by Gage Sparrow

Start scrolling!

Tyrannosaurus

The first dinosaur on my list is the Tyrannosaurus, or the T. Rex. I have always been a fan of the Tyrannosaurus because of it's enormous size and reputation for having been an apex predator of the time period. Throughout the Cretaceous period, the Tyrannosaurus roamed the lands of what is now Western North America, and has been estimated to have the highest bite force of any land animal. Today, the T. Rex is one of the most studied and most popular species of dinosaur of all time.

Troodon

My second favorite dinosaur, the Troodon, is also a land dwelling predator. It existed during the late Cretaceous period and was pretty small in size, reaching only about 110 pounds. To make up for its lack of size, the Troodon excelled in using its large brain and high intelligence to outsmart prey and survive in extremely hostile environments. In additiion to being uniquely intelligent, the troodon also possessed special, serrated teeth to aid in hunting and combat.

Iguanodon

The Iguanodon is a dinosaur that some may be unfamiliar with. In contrast to the previous two that have been listed, the Iguanodon is thought to have been a friendly herbivore! The Iguanodon was extremely large; ranging from 30-36 feet in length and weighing over 4 tons. They also possessed large, spiked thumbs that were useful for gathering resources and defending from predators.

Stegosaurus

Similar to the Iguanodon, the Stegosaurus was a massive herbivore who lived during the Late Jurassic period. Along the spine of the Stegosaurus existed huge, vertical plates of armor, while their tails stored large spikes used for defense. The Stegosaurus is also thought to have been friendly, with its diet likely consisting of mainly shrubs and bushes.

Diplodocus

Finally, the Diplodocus is my 5th favorite dinosaur. Existing during the late Kimmerdgian age, the Diplodocus was an extremely large herbivore. With a length of up to 100 feet, it has been thought to be one of the longest dinosaurs to ever roam the Earth. Most of its length comes from the extremely long tail, which can be made of up to 80 vertebrae!
